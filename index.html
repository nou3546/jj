JayJay

The excitement of growing up comes from the excitement of newness.
“First times” I would call them.
Dreams of someday having the freedom to drive a car wherever I wanted, or vacationing without the cumbersome weight of my family.
But now, with many drives, and many vacations, the used to be wonder factors have dulled, faded, from their fantastical status I once attributed to them.
No longer does my heart pump in excitement one month in advance of a vacation,
Nor in the advent of being a two hours drive away from home.

Even with my hobbies in high school, albeit some I may have been forced into,
But as college application season ended, and we moved onto college,
I considered my activities to be archived, never to be used again.
For me, they had no purpose, no value other than the attempt to please an admissions officer

Everything changed when I met you.
When I met you, it was like reliving my childhood.
Any activity, no matter how mediocre I previously thought it was, was raised to pure ecstasy if it meant that I was going to do it with you.
I believed that I would return home greeted by the dusty piano keys, rather, I play, in hopes of you singing along to the song.
Tennis balls which haven’t been used in over a year, was bounced on the court for the first time with you.
Activities that I would attribute to mundane: talking walks, cooking food, even rotting in bed, have become so enjoyable when you are by my side.
Jayjay, you have breathed new life into me.
You’ve made everything feel meaningful again.
I desire to do everything with you, from the most tedious tasks to highest highs, I want to do it all.

I dream of driving forever, talking while the radio plays.
I dream of exploring the world together with you, in any destination, in any part of the word.
You have no idea how much I want it.
You’ve given me a second life
Thank you, Sean
